Experiment 2 - Ebb and Flow of Life

Press p to pause the simulation. Press r to turn off random conditions. Press c to clear the grid. Click and drag to create and kill cells.

Description

The goal of this experiment was to try and put a new twist on Conway's game of life. I wanted for there to be an ebb and flow to the life, where there would be great blooms of cells, which would then slowly but surely die off. While the normal game of life is interesting, I hoped this would make it feel more alive.

Technical

The way it works is fairly simple. Using perlin noise, the program will decide if conditions are good for cells or if they are bad. If conditions are good, cells will begin to spawn in over the entire grid at random, based on how good things are. On the other hand, if conditions are bad, on the other hand, seemingly health cells will die at random. By using noise for this, the ebb and flow as good conditions create a bloom of cells and bad conditions cause most to die should feel natural. Unforunately, it is fairly intensive to simulate in p5.js, so not all of the canvas is covered.
